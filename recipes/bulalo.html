Bulalo

Description

Bulalo is beef shank soup with bone marrow still intact inside the bone. This is a very popular dish that is served in special occasions but you can order this dish in Filipino restaurants and fast foods all the time.

Ingredients

  • 1 and 1/2 kilo beef bulalo
  • 1 whole white onion
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3 pcs bay leaf
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns, cracked
  • salt
  • 2 bunches pechay
  • 1/2 head cabbage
  • 2 pcs corn in a cob
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, pounded and tied into a knot
  • 2 stalks leeks, cut into 1 inch
  • 3 pcs star anise
  • 1 thumbsized ginger, sliced
  • 1 beef cube, optional

Steps

  1. Place the beef shanks in a large casserole. Cover with water.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, bay leaf, star anise, ginger, lemongrass and peppercorns.
  3. Season with salt. Set over high heat and bring to a boil, removing scum as it rises.
  4. Lower the heat, cover and simmer for two hours or until the beef is fork-tender.
  5. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove the beef shanks and arrange at the center of individual soup bowls.
  6. Strain the broth. Reheat to boiling point. Cut corn into 3 pcs each.
  7. Core the cabbage and cut in half. Trim the ends of the pechay.
  8. Add the corn to the broth and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  9. Add the cabbage and pechay leaves and simmer for another 3 minutes.
  10. Scoop the vegetables out and arrange around the bulalo.
  11. Pour in hot broth and serve at once.
